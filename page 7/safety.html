Identification & First-Aid

Key physical differences between venomous Caucasian vipers and harmless water snakes, plus official emergency procedures in Georgia.

Viper vs. Harmless Snake Identification

⚠️ Caucasian Vipers (Viperidae)

Venomous Characteristics

  • Pupil Shape: Vertical slit-like pupils (like a cat's eye in bright light).
  • Head Shape: Distinctly broad, triangular, or heart-shaped head clearly offset from thin neck.
  • Body Proportion: Heavy-set, stocky, thick body with a short tail that tapers abruptly.
  • Scale Texture: Strongly keeled, rough-textured scales. Small scales covering the crown of head.
🛡️ Harmless Snakes (Colubridae)

Non-Venomous Characteristics

  • Pupil Shape: Perfectly round pupils (except the nocturnal Cat Snake).
  • Head Shape: Oval or oval-oblong head that blends smoothly into body without sharp neck taper.
  • Body Proportion: Slender, elongated body with long gradually tapering tail.
  • Head Plates: Large, smooth plate scales covering the top of the head.

Emergency First-Aid Protocol (Snakebite)

WHAT TO DO:

  1. Call 112 Immediately: Contact Georgian Emergency Response Service.
  2. Remain Calm: Panic increases heart rate and speeds up venom dissemination.
  3. Immobilize Limb: Keep bitten arm/leg still and below heart level.
  4. Remove Jewelry & Tight Clothing: Swelling can happen rapidly.
  5. Clean Wound: Gently wash with water and cover with clean dry cloth.

WHAT NOT TO DO:

  1. DO NOT Apply Tourniquet: Cuts off blood flow and exacerbates local tissue necrosis.
  2. DO NOT Cut or Suck Wound: Introduces mouth bacteria and does not extract venom.
  3. DO NOT Apply Ice: Causes severe frostbite and localized tissue damage.
  4. DO NOT Drink Alcohol or Caffeine: Increases systemic circulation.
  5. DO NOT Attempt to Catch Snake: Risk taking a second venomous bite.
